Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before getting covered.

What is pet insurance and how does it work? +

Pet insurance helps cover unexpected veterinary costs. You pay monthly premiums, visit any licensed vet when your pet needs care, pay the bill, then submit a claim to get reimbursed — typically 70–90% of covered costs.

Is iPet Insurance Services an insurance company? +

No — we are a licensed independent insurance agency in California. We represent multiple top-rated carriers and help you find the best plan for your pet and budget. We are not an insurer ourselves.

When should I enroll my pet? +

The sooner, the better! Most carriers have waiting periods (10–14 days for illness, 2–5 days for accidents), and pre-existing conditions are typically excluded. Enrolling while your pet is young and healthy gives you the widest coverage.

Are there any breed restrictions? +

All three of our partner carriers cover all dog breeds and cat breeds, including purebreds and mixed breeds. Some hereditary conditions may have specific waiting periods.

Can I use any veterinarian? +

Yes! All three of our carrier partners allow you to visit any licensed veterinarian or specialist in the United States and Canada — no network restrictions, ever.

How much does pet insurance cost? +

Plans typically start from $15–$30/month for dogs and $9–$20/month for cats, depending on breed, age, location, and coverage level. Contact us for a free personalized quote.

What is not covered by pet insurance? +

Pre-existing conditions, elective procedures, and cosmetic treatments are generally not covered. Routine/preventive care may require an optional wellness add-on.

How do I file a claim? +

Each carrier has an online portal or mobile app for claim submission. Simply upload your vet invoice and medical records. Claims are typically processed within 2–5 business days.

What Each Coverage Includes

Four detailed points under each coverage type — so you know exactly what you're protected against.

🤕

Accidents & Injuries

Coverage for sudden, unexpected physical harm caused by external events.

  • Broken bones, fractures, and sprains from falls, collisions, or trauma
  • Lacerations, bite wounds, and injuries requiring stitches or surgery
  • Foreign object ingestion requiring endoscopy or surgical removal
  • Poisoning or toxic ingestion — including emergency hospitalization and treatment
🦠

Illnesses & Infections

Covers a wide range of common and serious illness diagnoses and treatments.

  • Skin conditions, hot spots, fungal and bacterial skin infections
  • Ear infections, eye infections, and conjunctivitis treatment
  • Urinary tract infections (UTIs), bladder stones, and kidney disease
  • Respiratory illness, pneumonia, and upper respiratory infections
🔬

Diagnostics & Lab Tests

All the testing needed to accurately diagnose what's wrong with your pet.

  • Blood panels, urinalysis, fecal tests, and culture results
  • X-rays, ultrasounds, echocardiograms, and other imaging studies
  • MRI and CT scans for neurological and internal conditions
  • Biopsy, cytology, and pathology for cancer or tumor evaluation
🏥

Surgery & Hospitalization

Full surgical and inpatient care costs when your pet needs more intensive treatment.

  • Surgical procedures including anesthesia and operating room fees
  • Overnight stays and multi-day hospitalization at veterinary clinics
  • Post-operative care, follow-up exams, and wound management
  • Specialist referral fees for surgery centers and veterinary hospitals
💊

Prescription Medications

Covers the cost of prescription drugs needed to treat covered conditions.

  • Antibiotics and antifungals prescribed by a licensed veterinarian
  • Anti-inflammatory, pain management, and steroid medications
  • Chemotherapy drugs and specialty oncology medications
  • Chronic disease medications for thyroid, diabetes, and heart conditions
🧬

Hereditary & Congenital Conditions

Breed-specific and genetic conditions that manifest during your pet's lifetime.

  • Hip and elbow dysplasia in large breeds like Labs and German Shepherds
  • Heart conditions such as mitral valve disease and cardiomyopathy
  • Cataracts, progressive retinal atrophy, and other eye disorders
  • Brachycephalic syndrome in flat-faced breeds such as Bulldogs and Pugs
🦷

Dental Illness

Coverage for dental diseases — genuine dental health issues, not cosmetic cleaning.

  • Tooth extractions for severely infected or broken teeth
  • Gingivitis treatment and deep periodontal cleaning procedures
  • Periodontal disease management and jaw bone infections
  • Fractured or chipped teeth causing pain or secondary infection
🎗️

Cancer Treatment

Comprehensive cancer care from diagnosis all the way through treatment and follow-up.

  • Chemotherapy infusions, oral chemotherapy, and associated medications
  • Radiation therapy sessions at veterinary oncology centers
  • Surgical tumor removal and biopsy procedures
  • Imaging, staging tests, and specialist oncology consultations
🚑

Emergency & Specialty Care

After-hours emergencies and specialist referrals are fully included.

  • Emergency room visits at 24/7 veterinary emergency hospitals
  • Specialist referrals: cardiologists, neurologists, dermatologists
  • Physical and hydrotherapy rehabilitation after injury or surgery
  • Oxygen therapy, IV fluids, blood transfusions in critical care
🌡️

Chronic Conditions

Long-term management of ongoing medical conditions that require continuous care.

  • Diabetes management including insulin, monitoring supplies, and vet visits
  • Arthritis treatment with NSAIDs, joint supplements, and physical therapy
  • Allergy diagnosis, immunotherapy shots, and allergy management plans
  • Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism medication and ongoing monitoring
✨

Wellness Add-Ons (Optional)

Optional preventive care riders available from all three carrier partners.

  • Annual wellness exams and routine physical checkups with your vet
  • Core and non-core vaccinations (rabies, DHPP, Bordetella, and more)
  • Flea, tick, and heartworm prevention medications and treatments
  • Spay/neuter procedures and dental cleaning reimbursements
